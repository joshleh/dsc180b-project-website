Why Pulmonary Edema Matters

Pulmonary edema is a serious medical condition where fluid builds up in the lungs, making it difficult to breathe. Doctors currently rely on chest X-rays and written radiology reports to detect it, but these signs can be subtle, easy to miss, and interpreted differently across clinicians. When detection is delayed, patients can deteriorate quickly — making early and accurate recognition critical.

Our project explores whether modern AI tools can support clinicians by helping identify signs of pulmonary edema more quickly and consistently. Building on our earlier work, we developed a system that analyzes both medical images and clinical text, with the goal of highlighting important visual patterns and summarizing key findings in a clear, interpretable way. The goal isn’t to replace expert judgment, but to create tools that can help doctors make faster and more informed decisions in high-risk situations.
